# Bitcoin Market Analysis — April 18, 2026

---

## Bottom Line Up Front

Bitcoin surged from ~$74,500 to an intraday peak of $78,300 after Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz "completely open" on April 17 — then fell back to ~$76,000 within 24 hours as the IRGC re-closed it. The move was a liquidation-driven short squeeze ($762M in crypto liquidations, $382M BTC shorts), not an organic breakout. The Hormuz situation is the dominant market driver and remains unresolved: the ceasefire expires in ~5 days, the U.S. blockade of Iranian ports is still in force, and Iran won't keep the strait open while the blockade holds. On-chain signals remain the strongest they've been all cycle — but the macro override (stagflation, frozen Fed, oil near $94-99) is real and can move faster than any on-chain signal.

## Geopolitical Situation Update — The Full Hormuz Timeline

This is the only story that matters right now.

**Background:** On February 28, 2026, the U.S. and Israel launched coordinated strikes on Iran, assassinating Supreme Leader Khamenei. The IRGC immediately closed the Strait of Hormuz — through which ~20% of global oil flows. Global oil output fell ~10.1 mb/d. Physical crude briefly spiked above $150/bbl. Brent averaged $103/bbl in March; IEA projects a Q2 peak of $115/bbl.

**April 8:** Pakistan-mediated 2-week ceasefire announced → BTC rallied to a 3-week high.

**April 12:** Peace talks collapsed → Trump announced a formal U.S. naval blockade of Iranian ports → BTC and crypto sold off.

**April 17 — The Relief Rally:**
- Iran's FM Araghchi declared the strait "completely open to all commercial shipping"
- Trump simultaneously claimed Iran agreed to "unlimited suspension" of its nuclear program
- Brent crude plunged **~9% to ~$90/bbl**; WTI dropped **~11.4% to ~$83.85**
- BTC broke from $74,500 → **$78,300** — triggering a massive short squeeze
- **$762M in crypto liquidations** ($382M BTC shorts, $167M ETH shorts)
- Broader crypto market cap rose 3.79% to $2.77 trillion

**April 18 — The Re-Closure:**
- IRGC announced control of the strait "returned to its previous state" — effectively re-closed within 24 hours
- State news agency Nour confirmed the strait is back under "strict management and control by armed forces"
- Two tanker owners reported receiving Iranian radio transmissions shutting the waterway; one supertanker reported gunfire and aborted transit
- Iran's parliament speaker: the strait will not remain open while the U.S. blockade of Iranian ports persists
- Trump: the blockade "will remain in full force until our transaction with Iran is 100% complete"
- BTC fell back from $78,300 → **~$76,000** in Asian hours

**Current status:** The ceasefire technically remains active but expires in approximately **5 days (around April 22)**. The fundamental deadlock is unresolved — the U.S. won't lift the port blockade; Iran won't open Hormuz while the blockade holds. The window for a deal is narrow.

**Oil today:** After the -11% drop on April 17, Brent partially recovered on the re-closure news. Now trading ~$94-99/bbl — far above pre-war $65-70, far below the $150 spike peak.

**Stagflation status:** Pre-war Feb CPI was 2.4%. March CPI: **~3.3%** — energy-driven. Dallas Fed has quantified the oil-to-CPI pass-through. IEA projects Brent peaking at $115/bbl in Q2. The FOMC meets April 28-29 — the central question is no longer when they cut, but whether they can cut at all in 2026.

**Wildcard:** Iran floated collecting Hormuz transit tolls in Bitcoin. No formal policy, but not nothing.

## Net Read

BTC is trapped in a Hormuz headline loop. The on-chain architecture is the most constructive it has been all cycle — but every time the market tries to price in a geopolitical resolution, Iran re-closes the strait. The fundamental deadlock (U.S. won't lift port blockade → Iran won't open Hormuz) has not been resolved, and the ceasefire expires around April 22. That date is the next binary. A genuine deal → oil falls → CPI eases → Fed cut path reopens → BTC squeezes toward $83-87K. Ceasefire collapse → oil back toward $115 → March CPI + energy shock + Fed paralysis → BTC retests $72-74K or lower.

The 200-day MA at $87,500 is the real target for a confirmed trend reversal — and it's still 12-14% overhead. The $76K-$78K zone is interesting but not yet structurally secured. Watch the **April 22 ceasefire deadline** and the **April 28-29 FOMC** as the two events most likely to resolve the current standoff.

For accumulation: the on-chain case for staged buying remains intact. But sizing should reflect that the geopolitical situation can override any chart signal on a 24-hour news cycle.
